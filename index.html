Smithsonian's National Portrait Gallery

The Smithsonian's National Portrait Gallery tells the history of America through individuals who have shaped its culture.

- Smithsonian's National Portait Gallery    




On the website by Google Arts & Culture, numerous collections of historical artwork and images are available to explore. Another feature on the website is the virtual tour of the Smithsonian's National Portrait Gallery located in Washington, D.C. The tour can be found here: Virtual Tour


There are multiple floors of the museum, each with different exhibits. The first floor holds the "Experience America" and "Votes for Women" exhibits, where many of the displays date back to before the 1940s. The second floor contains the "America's Presidents" exhibit which includes artwork and pieces depicting the United States presidents.

Experience America

The "Experience America" exhibit displays many paintings from a pilot program in 1934 introduced during Franklin Roosevelt's term. The purpose of the program was to have artists from around the nation find ways to capture the beauty of America in light of the Great Depression. Many of the paitings were displayed in schools, post offices, and other public goverment controlled buildings.

Votes for Women: A Portrait of Persistence

The "Votes for Women" exhibit displays portaits of women of all races who played crucial roles during the movement for women's rights between 1832 and 1920. It also includes some flyers and other artifacts from the time period relating to women's suffrage, providing a glimpse into the inner workings of the movement.

America's Presidents

The "America's Presidents" exhibit showcases portraits of the past presidents of the United States, each with a short summary of their life and contributions to the nation. There are other pieces of various mediums, like sculptures and artifacts, that are also included in the display.
